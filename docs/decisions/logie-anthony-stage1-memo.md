### Why Ratio Analysis Matters  
Financial statements, income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements provide important information and raw data to create meaningful strategic evaluations; however, they do not connect key figures or show how performance has changed within the company over time.
Ratio analysis can extend that data, enabling us to make quick and informed descion making within the company. 
Ratio analysis grants us the ability to evaluate Caterpillar's (CAT) Financial performance, efficiency, leverage, and short term liquidity in ways that are standardized and comparable over the foreseeable future. 
Ratio analysis highlights trends, identifies strengths and weaknesses, and reveals drivers of profitability that may not be immediately apparent in standardized financial statements.
Specifically, ratio analysis in a way makes a clear view of Caterpillar's profitability by analyzing how effectively and efficiently Caterpillar converts its revenue into earnings. 
To provide some example challenge elements, not limited to, but including elements such as declining margins, slowing asset turnover, or rising debt levels, can be identified much earlier through the use of ratio analysis compared to standard financial statement analysis. 
Moving towards ratio analysis will lead to deeper levels of insight, enabling more informed strategic and financially beneficial decision making.

### Six Ratio Categories  
- **Performance Ratios:** Measure overall effectiveness and how well resources can generate revenue. 
- **Profitability Ratios:** Evaluate the company’s ability to generate earnings in correlation to revenue, operating costs, assets, and equity. 
- **Efficiency Ratios:**  Measure how effectively assets and liabilities are managed. 
- **Leverage Ratios:** Analyze the company’s ability to meet long-term obligations and the extent of debt financing.
- **Liquidity Ratios:** Analyze the company’s ability to meet short-term obligations.
- **DuPont Analysis:** Decomposes return on equity into profitability, efficiency, and leverage components.
